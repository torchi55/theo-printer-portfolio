Farm to Brick

In second-semester studio, we were tasked with designing a farmers market pavilion drawing inspiration from four related objects of our choosing. Drawn to LEGO — and specifically to the limitless potential hidden within a bin of loose pieces — this design isolates and elevates four individual components: a LEGO head, torso, legs, and jackhammer. Each is extracted from the chaotic mass to emphasize its singular form and significance, physically suspended within a structured wire framework.

The program stacks a multi-level farmers market above a ground plane of food-truck parking. Stalls, retail spaces, and green areas each occupy an exactly defined cubic volume on a continuous modular grid. Much like the way LEGO bricks snap into place, these units are designed for flexibility, adaptation, and reconfiguration — a playful, ever-evolving architecture.
