A1.00 PROGRAM + GOALS

The Pike Courtyard system is built around two constraints: a narrow rectangular yard and two existing palm trees. Every element — the risers, the tower, the stage — responds to those palms rather than fighting them. The goal was a space that could scale from a 50-person hang to a 1,000-person headline show without structural compromise.

LINEWEIGHT PLAN · FULL COURTYARD RENDER
  • 01
    Modular Riser Bay × 2

    Stepped timber risers with 4×4 DF posts and 2×4 framing. Mirrored across the courtyard for symmetry. Each bay supports up to 4,000 lbs — enough for a packed row at any event.

    REF A-201
  • 02
    King Tower

    Built-in bar below the tower deck. Two service points — one per courtyard side — aimed to split crowd pressure and prevent the single-bar bottleneck that kills event flow.

    REF A-301
  • 03
    Performer Platform

    Elevated DJ platform, raised above the crowd and visible from every position in the courtyard. Central focus for the performance. Can run as the primary stage or as a second DJ stand for back-to-back sets when the main stage needs a changeover.

    REF A-401
  • 04
    Courtyard Programme

    Circulation. Spread people out of the main dance floor and onto the risers — capacity of 1,000+ means the space needs clear paths for large-scale events like Diplo.

    REF A-501

A3.00 · Focal Programme Node

King
Tower.

Two programme elements stacked vertically, built around the existing palm. Hover the drawing or the list to inspect each callout.

  • 01
    Dual Bar Service

    Bar service below the tower deck — one counter per courtyard side. Splitting the crowd across two service points prevents the single-bar bottleneck. Both bars face outward into the courtyard wings, so traffic flows away from the centre.

  • 02
    Palm Tree

    Retained landscape element. The tower is designed around it, not despite it — the palm becomes part of the tectonic, not an obstacle to it.

  • 03
    Performer Platform

    Elevated DJ platform at the base of the tower. Acts as a second performance deck when the main stage is changing over, or a high-visibility vantage point. Sightlines extend across the full courtyard from this position.
